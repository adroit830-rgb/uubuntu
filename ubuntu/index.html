Linux serves as the foundation for Ubuntu, a free and open-source operating system. It is one of the most widely used Linux distributions for PCs, servers, and cloud computing, developed by Canonical Ltd.

Because of its reputation for being easy to use, it's a fantastic option for beginners who wish to learn more about Linux.

Advantages

Feature Explanation
Free & Open Source No cost to use; full access to modify code.
Secure Minimal risk of malware or viruses.
Stable & Reliable Rarely crashes and updates are consistent.
Lightweight Runs smoothly even on old or low-spec systems.
Software Center Easy to install apps and updates via GUI.
Customizable Can change the look, feel, and behavior easily.
Developer-friendly Great for Python, C/C++, Java, and web development.
Regular Updates New LTS (Long-Term Support) versions every 2 years.
